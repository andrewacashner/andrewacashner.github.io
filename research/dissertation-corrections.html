If you find a possible error in my dissertation, please contact me (cashner at usc dot edu) so that I may post a correction here. For the musical editions listed below, if you would like to use these for study or performance, please contact me so that I can provide you with a corrected edition.

Edition of Salazar's Angélicos coros con gozo cantad

  • (2016/02/08) In m. 20, the fourth pitch in the Bassus II part should be G2, not F2.
  • In m. 66, after Copla 2, there should be a marking D. C. al Fine, with the Fine at the end of m. 46.

Edition of Padilla’s Voces, las de la capilla

  • (2015/10/05) Stephen Black of the University of Southern California identified several errors of pitches and part labels in the edition of Juan Gutiérrez de Padilla’s Voces, las de la capilla.
  • In musical example 3.5 on page 178, the fourth pitch in the Tenor I voice should be D4, the same as the third note.

Edition of Cáseda's Qué música divina

  • (2015/10/13) Luis Reyes Duarte of the University of Southern California identified a pitch error in the edition of José de Cáseda's Qué música divina: the second note of the Tenor part in m. 4 should be F, not E.

Edition of Padilla's Al establo más dichoso

  • (2016/02/08) In mm. 59–69, the underlaid text of the second stanza is incorrect (it is erroneously repeated from the choral introduction just preceding). The correct second stanza is given in the edition of the poetry, lines 41--44 on p. 516 of the dissertation.
  • In m. 111, the fourth pitch in the Bassus II part should be G3, not B3.
  • In m. 118, the Tiple I lyrics should begin Turuturuyega, not Suruturuyega.
