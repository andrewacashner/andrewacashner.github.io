Claudio Monteverdi, L’Orfeo

Musical Elements

17C theory, notation

  • Clefs: treble (G2), soprano (C1), alto (C3), tenor (C4), bass (F4)
  • “Key signatures”: cantus durus (all naturals), cantus mollis (one flat)
    • cantus mollis = cantus durus transposed up P4 (down P5)
      • e.g., mode I (was on D), now it’s on G with one-flat signature
  • Hexachords (Guidonian solmization): (ut, re, mi, fa, sol, la)
    1. C: naturalis (natural)
    2. G: durus (hard)
    3. F: mollis (soft)
  • 8 modes: (medieval)
    1. D final, authentic ambitus (D -> D+8)
    2. D final, plagal ambitus (A -> A+8) 3 E authentic 4 E plagal 5 F authentic 6 F plagal 7 G authentic 8 G plagal
  • -> 12 modes c. 16C (Zarlino) 9/10 A 11/12 C polyphonic music: final + ambitus of the Tenor

(church keys, tonoi)

Bs: hard (durus) B (natural), soft (mollis) B (flat)

Basso continuo

Left hand plays bass line, right hand plays “consonances and dissonances above the bass according to the figures” (JS Bach) - default: 8/5/3 intervals above bass

Music printing

  • Engraving - precise, elegant, expensive - hand drawn/carved
  • Double impression printing - 1st impression: staff lines, 2nd notes
  • Single impression printing - complete type block for each note in each staff position

Genre, style

Recitative - solo singer, cantar parlando (singing while speaking) with instrumental accompaniment, primarily chordal

- Monody - whole piece in recitative
