The Lutheran Movement

  • Law and Gospel
    • Law: “you’re a sinner and can’t help yourself”
    • Gospel (good news): “Christ is your savior and his righteousness counts for you.”
    • we are saved by God’s grace along (free, unmerited gift) through faith alone in Christ alone
    • we know this on the basis of Scripture alone
  • 1517 - Martin Luther’s 95 Theses on indulgences and justification
    • Catholics interpret Luther as attacking church’s authority, giving people up to their own feelings
  • Lutherans supported by German princes for political reasons as well as spiritual ones
  • important role of printing, reading

Principles of liturgical reform

  • Luther: a conservative reformer
  • “abuse does not merit disuse”
  • “everything that is not forbidden in Scripture may be permitted so long as it does not obscure the Gospel”
  • music is a gift of God, it glorifies God

  • also helps the Word reach people

  • Relationship to tradition
  • Relationship to other denominations

  • Community structure

  • Times and patterns of worship
    • Sunday Gottesdienst (divine service/liturgy)
    • Matins and Vespers (morning/evening prayer)
    • city churches with Latin schools vs. parish churches
  • example: Hamburg
    • personnel: priest/pastor, deacon (front); organist (back); Kantor (choir director) (back/middle, balcony?)
    • three-hour service
      1. “service of the word”
        • Organ praeludium
        • Organ intro to the introit
        • Introit in Latin (boys)
        • Opening hymn: Komm, heiliger Geist (Come Holy Spirit) with organ intro, interludes
        • Kyrie (Greek, boys: chant, polyphony) with organ intro
        • Luther’s Kyrie hymn in German with organ intro
        • Gloria (boys, Latin, polyphonic)
        • Luther’s Gloria hymn in German Allein Gott in der Höh’ sei Ehr with organ, choir alternatim
        • Readings (German) with Gradual, Alleluia
          • between readings: motet
        • Hymn of the day (proper to the day, thematic)
          • e.g., Easter: Christ lag in Todesbanden
          • e.g., Reformation: Ein’ feste Burg ist unser Gott
      2. sermon
      3. “service of the table”
        • Creed + Creed hymn
        • communion prayers and chants
        • Sanctus + Sanctus hymn
        • Agnus Dei + communion hymn/devotional song (solo, choir piece)
        • Benedictus

  • personnel: three main churches in Hamburg (likewise in Leipzig under JS Bach in 18C), only one school choir; kantor rotates, organist fills in when choir is not there

  • Stadtpfeiffer - town band

Genres of Lutheran Music in Church, School, and Home

  • Traditional masses, motets: Latin or vernacular
  • Chorales (hymns) and chorale-based composition (in alternatim)
    • voices
    • organ chorale preludes
  • Organ praeludium (pl., praeludia) – free, free/imitative
  • Spruchmotette (Spruch, saying), e.g., “I am the Resurrection and the Life”
    • reiterate Gospel message and give people Trost (comfort of salvation)
  • Polychoral motets
  • Sacred concerto
  • Concerto with aria

  • Cantata: sections with recitative and aria

  • Hausandacht - home devotions led by Hausvater

  • much fewer opportunities for women

“The Musical Practice of Piety”

  • “The New Piety” (Frömmigkeitsbewegung)
  • Praxis pietatis melica, Crüger and Gerhardt – “the musical practice of piety”

  • contrast with earlier devotio moderna, later Pietism

  • religion of the heart, led to a religion of the hands
  • individual, inward-centered piety, personal relationship of love with Jesus

  • partly developed in response to devastating 1618-48 Thirty Years’ War
