Musical consolidation

(examples of new things in this period)

  • violins
  • virtuosity
  • sonata
  • partita
  • keys and tonality
  • harmonic movement: departure and return
  • - sequences
  • - larger-scale tonal planning
  • cantata
  • recitative–aria pairs – opera, church music
  • more continuity and flow – one movement, one style, one “feel”

Political consolidation

  • rise of absolute monarchies in France and Spain
    • Philip IV of Spain
    • Louis XIV of France
  • expansion of European colonial empires
    • French, Spanish, British, Austrians
  • increasing regimentation of daily life, increasing state control of daily affairs
  • also increasing sense of individuality

National opera experiments

  • opera vs. established traditions of spoken theater (with songs)
  • musical theater as demonstration of royal sovereignty, power

France

Lully - court composer to Louis XIV of France - royal monopoly on opera composition - develops first French operas

Spain

Hidalgo - court harpist for Philip IV of Spain - writes first Spanish operas (also the last Spanish operas) - Torrejón y Velasco sets La púrpura as first opera in New World, Lima 1700 (1710?) - (zarzuela – musical theater featuring spoken dialogue and songs)

England

Purcell - England - writes first English operas
