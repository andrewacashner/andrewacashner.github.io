Spanish musical theater and opera

  • war with France, resolved: Peace of the Pyrenees (1659)
  • 1660 - first Spanish operas - connected to French-Spanish wedding
  • Celos aún del aire matan, La púrpura de la rosa [music lost]
    • mythological cautionary tales against unfaithfulness in marriage
  • opera was working against strong tradition of spoken theater with songs
    • by Lope de Vega, Calderón
    • emphasis on realism
  • libretti by Calderón
  • fully sung opera preceded by “semi-operas” by Hidalgo/Calderón (including sacred drama, auto sacramental)

  • polystylism - human characters sing actual songs; non-human characters sing in ~recitative; also lots of choral music

  • parallel tradition of zarzuela, musical theater with spoken dialogue and songs

  • example from Celos: “Noble en Tinacria naciste”
    • music alludes to the style of jácara to depict character “from the streets”
    • street ballad about outlaw heros
    • cf. sacred jácaras earlier on syllabus (Juan Gutiérrez de Padilla, A la jácara jacarilla)
      • strong theatrical element of Spanish church music
    • guitar accompaniment - realism: diegetic song (it’s a song in the world of the show)

  • a lot of interplay among oral traditions, folk/popular music, culto cultivated, “civilized”, notated, complex music

A la jácara (outlaw ballad), jacarilla
de buen garbo y lindo porte
traigo por plato de corte
siendo pasto de la villa

(“I bring as a dish of the court what is really feed from the village”)

English musical theater

  • similar situation w/r/t spoken theater traditions (Shakespeare!)

  • Henry Purcell and others developing “semi-opera” with more music

  • experiment in fully-sung opera, Purcell’s Dido and Aeneas

  • example: “Come Fellow Sailors”
    • warnings against men
  • first performed at a girls’ boarding school, c. 1680

  • based on Virgil’s Aeneid

  • Dido’s Lament
    • ground bass - repeated bass line
    • repeated set of harmonies (modern: changes)
    • descending tetrachord – “lament bass”

French opera

  • Jean-Baptiste Lully
    • Italian
    • childhood friends with Louis XIV
    • given monopoly on opera
    • (Ballard given monopoly on printing music)
  • strong traditions - spoken drama (Racine)
  • Neoclassical interest - esp. mythology

  • dance (ballet)

  • tragèdie en musique
  • integrating serious drama, dance, choral
  • style of recitative based on cadence of French language

  • spectacle of royal power
