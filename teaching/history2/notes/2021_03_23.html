Bach to schedule

Dieterich Buxtehude, Praeludium in E

  • “Preluding” - style of improvisation
    • introducting things in church service, transitions, interludes
    • free style (vs. strict contrapuntal style)
  • alternating free and strict/contrapuntal style
    • free: stilus fantasticus (fantasy)
    • fugue: one voice following after another (fuga = “flight”, cf. “catch”)

Fugue

- strict counterpoint, distinct voices
- each voice independent; premium on contrary motion, imitation but at
  different times
- "subject" - main theme; "answer" - repeats theme displaced by some
  interval (often 5th)
    - "real answer" - exactly same intervals, rhythm as subject
    - "tonal answer" - modified to fit demands of tonality

Models

  • alternation of free and fugal

  • “Variation toccata” (exx. Frescobaldi, Froberger)

  • rhetoric (Lena Jacobsen)
    • Classical (Greek/Roman) - Cicero, Quintilian
    1. Exordium
      • call to attention, basic introduction of the theme
      • bold gesture
    2. Propositio
      • logical main statement of your theme
    3. Narratio
      • Confutatio - entertain objection
      • Confirmatio - defend, restate your argument
    4. Peroratio
      • rousing conclusion
      • call to action

Bach, Prelude and Fugue in A minor

  • “Preluding” and “fuguing” sections now separated into full movements
    • typical of High Baroque tendency to have one consistent mood/affect/character per movement and to have larger structures made possible by tonal harmonic organization
  • Bach’s style: Bigger, longer, more notes, more chromatic notes, more tension, more angular and weird
  • Incorporating the modern violin instrumental style (Vivaldi) and harmonic language (Corelli, Vivaldi) into more traditional genres (organ prelude/fugue, cantata, etc.)
    • doing a new thing in an old way (or doing an old thing in a new way?)
