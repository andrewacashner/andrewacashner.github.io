Music and/in/as culture

Music not only reflects social structures but actively shapes them.

Eighteenth-century opera and power/politics

Italian opera seria (serious opera)

  • “Son qual nave” and Farinelli the castrato
    • Riccardo Broschi; brother Carlo
    • superhuman and subhuman - paradigm of celebrities
    • messa di voce < > on single note
  • reform movement led by the poet Metastasio
    • make opera serious again
  • protagonist = sovereign, king torn between love and duty

  • endless series of recitative–da-capo aria pairs

  • Martha Feldman, Opera and Sovereignty
    • opera seria = ritual embodiment/enactment of sovereign power

  • society on stage actively shaping society off stage

French tragédie en musique and opéra-ballet

- opera-ballet: medley of scenes

Italian opera buffa (comic opera)

  • reintroduce comic elements through intermezzi

  • between acts of opera seria

  • Naples, 1730s
    • conservatorios - orphanages focused on musical training
    • partimenti - stock voice-leading patterns built above a bass line
      • Robert Gjerdingen, Music in the Galant Style
  • Pergolesi, La serva padrona
    • originally intermezzo, later a standalone work
    • servant girl who tricks her noble master into falling in love with her (~princess)
  • opera buffa - fantasies of bourgeois social mobility
    • developing new conventions for emotional expression
    • more flexible, moment-to-moment “realistic/naturalistic” expression
    • galant style

  • exx. Leonardo Vinci, Baldassare Galuppi, Johann Adolf Hasse

Influence of Italian comic opera in France

  • La serva padrona goes to Paris in 1750s
  • pamphlet war featuring Jean-Jacques Rousseau

  • “Querelle des bouffons” (clown quarrel, buffoon battle)

  • proxy battle about power and privilege

“Reform” opera

Christoph Willibald Gluck - wants to synthesize all the above traditions - opposed to conventionality - Orfeo opening, “Eurydice!” - Rousseau, cri animal de passion (soul’s cry of passion)

Opera as slavery

  • Minette and Lise in Haiti before revolution (1805)

  • Praskovia in Russia, “The Pearl” (serf opera)

  • women depicting stories of social mobility that were denied them in reality
