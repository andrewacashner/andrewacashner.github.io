Listening Guide

Handel's Messiah and Mozart's Arrangement

Aria, O Thou That Tellest Good Tidings to Zion

General questions to consider

  • How does Mozart pay homage to Handel?
  • What does Mozart do that was old-fashioned in his time, and what that was new?
  • (The really big-picture question) How was the Western canon created? What defines the Classical tradition in music?

Handel's version, London, 1741

  • What genre is Messiah?
  • What is the basic formal structure of this aria?
  • What are the main characteristics of its style? What about this is typical of High Baroque style? What seems more like Classical style?
  • Identify three main sections.
    • For the first section, analyze the harmony and phrasing together, using only I, V, and V/V (ignoring transpositions and passing chords).
  • Identify instances of word painting and general affective expression.
  • Match up recurring motives from the ritornello with the verbal phrases to which they are set.

Mozart's version, Vienna, 1789

  • If you were Mozart, what features of the music would you want to emphasize, adapt, or mitigate? What elements would be most interesting to you?
  • What does Mozart keep the same and awhat does he add? Look at instrumentation (make sure you know how to read the transposing parts), articulation, structure?
  • What does he highlight or downplay?
  • Do his additions change the character of the piece?
  • Does this version lend itself to a different performing style?
