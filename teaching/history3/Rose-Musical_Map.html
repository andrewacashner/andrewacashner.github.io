Stephen Rose, The Musical Map of Europe, c. 1700

(CH18CM)

General tips

  • Print and read on paper; underline and make notes in pencil (plus highlighter if that helps)

Reading questions

General

  • Print out a blank map of Europe (empty, or with modern national borders). Fill in the cities, nations, and regions mentioned in the text. (You might also explore making custom maps, which can include itineraries, in Google Maps).
    • Which places does the author mention most frequently?
    • What does he leave off?
  • How does the chapter expand on the quote from Charles Burney on p. 3?
  • What kinds of primary sources does the author, Stephen Rose, use as evidence?

Specific

Types of musical environment
  • (Pp. 3-4) Why did musicians travel?
  • (4-5) What is a court?
  • What were the advantages and disadvantages of court employment for musicians?
  • Name three prominent composers who worked as court musicians, and where.
  • What is the public sphere? What were the public venues for music-making?
  • Who came to different types of concerts?
  • What kind of performers made the most money?
  • (9-10) What is a patron? How did they support musicians?
  • What kinds of things could you read in early music periodicals? Who was the audience for these publications?
National styles
  • (11) What main national styles does the author describe?
  • What were the stereotypical differences between styles?
  • Give examples of composers who developed a mixed style.
  • (16) What were the advantages of travelling to Italy?
