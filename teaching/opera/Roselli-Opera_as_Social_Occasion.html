Reading Guide

John Roselli, Opera as a Social Occasion

Parker, Oxford Illustrated History of Opera, ch. 12

General

  • How has the significance of opera as a social event in a particular community changed?
  • What were some of the reasons people of different sorts attended the opera, other than to hear music or see a drama?

Specific

  • (454) How did the arrangement and behavior of the audience in court opera reflect the hierarchical social relationships of aristocratic society?
  • (458) What were the boxes used for?
  • (460) What were some distinguishing features of French opera houses and opera-goers as opposed to Italian ones?
  • (462) How did people find out about opera?
  • (465) What are some of the different types of people you would see working at the opera?
  • Describe the range of normal audience behaviors before the late 19th century. Consider dress, hygiene, recreation, and food.
  • (477) How did audiences and performers communicate with each other?
  • What did Richard Wagner change about the opera experience?
  • What does the author, John Roselli, believe we have lost in the experience of opera in recent years? Do you think the trends he describes are still relevant today, since this was written in 1994?
