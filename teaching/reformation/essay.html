Final Exam, Take-Home Essay

The primary point of debate between Reformation churches was the meaning of the death and resurrection of Jesus: how it affected individual people and the whole community, what it meant for how they should live. The practices of worship, including music, of these traditions emphasized their distinctive beliefs and attitudes toward this central element of Christianity.

The music listed below is all connected in some way with beliefs and rituals related to Christ’s death and resurrection, each from one post-Reformation tradition. Choose three selections and write an essay that uses these three examples to explain the characteristic patterns of worship in these traditions. The essay should be about four pages in length (double-spaced, standard format). First determine which tradition the pieces belong to and identify the sources of the texts (direct or indirect).

Consider questions like these:

  • How do the pieces build on or interpret their Scriptural or other sources?
  • What are the most significant similarities and differences in theology, musical style, mood/affect?
  • How do these pieces reflect the community’s beliefs about music’s role in worship?
  • What do they suggest about the individual person’s response to Jesus, or the community’s response?
  • Above all, how did these pieces of music, in the context of community worship, structure and reflect the beliefs and values of the community?

Academic Integrity

This is a take-home, open-book essay. You may use any sources to help you. You must cite every source you use, including full publication information, URL, etc.

This must be your own work. You may not collaborate in any way with other students in this class or outside of class.

It is not a research project, though. You should be able to find the basic information about your piece fairly quickly online and using existing course materials. The main goal is for you to analyze and interpret the example within the framework of the traditions as we have learned them in this course, and to give a sense of your knowledge and understanding of worship and music in those traditions, how they are distinct and how they relate to each other.
