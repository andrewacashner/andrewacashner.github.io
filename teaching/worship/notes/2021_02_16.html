Voices from the early church

  • AD 70 - Jewish revolt, Romans destroyed Jerusalem

  • Emmaus story (Luke 24) - two disciples walking with Christ (as a stranger); Christ explains how the OT points to him; then they share a meal and he is “made known to them in the breaking of the bread”
    • Reading and discussing/proclaiming Scripture, looking for Christ in the Scriptures
    • Communal meal, with ceremony of breaking bread, giving thanks, sharing bread and cup
      • coming to know/share in the presence of the risen Jesus
    • -> “Service of Word and Table”

Justin Martyr

  • Scripture
    • looking for Christ in prophecies of OT
  • Baptism
    • metaphors, images:
      • “dedicated ourselves to God”
      • washing
      • “made new” through Christ: regeneration
      • rebirth – explicit connecting to childbirth
      • illumination (photismos)
      • “choice and knowledge”
      • purity - “white as snow”
      • remission of sins
      • initation into group
    • practices, forms:
      • “go where there is water” (outdoors, running water)
      • adult baptism the norm
      • preparation period of prayer and fasting – putting sin behind you
      • preceded by instruction, conviction of faith, determination to live in the Christian way

      • “in the name of the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit” (but one God)

      • connection to (Apostles’) Creed, confession of faith – Trinitarian (but not formally defined)

  • Eucharist
    • metaphors, images
      • bread => flesh, wine => blood (but how)?
        • “are”
        • comparison/analogy to Incarnation
          • Jesus taking on flesh
        • to eating, nutrition
      • remembering Christ
      • cites words of Jesus at the Last Supper
      • 1 Corinthians (traditio, handing over)
    • practices
      • baptism of adult believers leading directly into Eucharist
      • “hearty prayers in common”
      • kiss of peace after prayers, before communion
      • bring bread and wine (and water)
      • “president” - leader with a special role
      • gives thanks over the bread and wine (connection to Birkat hamazon)
      • Amen (“so be it”)
      • partake of bread and wine
  • Weekly worship
    • Sunday, first day of week
      • connection to creation (Gen 1:1)
      • Jesus rose from dead
        • NOT the Christian sabbath
        • Sunday worship NOT a “reenactment of the Last Supper”
    • gather in one place
    • read “memoirs” of the apostles OR the prophets as long as time allows (OT and NT)
    • instruction and exhortation (preaching) & Amen
    • communion/Eucharist
    • offering for the poor, needy, “strangers sojourning among us” – service to those in need seen as integral to Christian worship
