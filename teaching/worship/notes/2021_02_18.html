Can we reconstruct “The Apostolic Tradition”?

  • there is NOT one “Apostolic Tradition”
  • many local practices, changing over time, consolidated later

  • diversity of practice, belief

  • for our purposes: focus more on change and adaptation than on the establishment of some pristine tradition

Musical legacies

  • Oxyrhynchus papyrus (3rd century)

  • possible ancient Sanctus preserved in medieval Requiem mass

  • both suggest Christian worship music was simple, easy to sing along with, syllabic (one note per syllable most of the time)

  • but legacies of Jewish worship and Christian traditions most closely connected to them like the Syrian Orthodox or “Oriental” churches in Iraq, Georgia, etc., suggest a more elaborate form of musical worship

  • Syrian Orthodox practices today
