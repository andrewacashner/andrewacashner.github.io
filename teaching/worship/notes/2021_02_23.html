Review - “Building Blocks” of apostolic traditions

The Impact of Legalization and Entanglement with Worldly Power

  • Place and space
    • from house churches to basilicas
      • Roman public buildings with open plan - rectangular
      • inheriting buildings from worldly power
      • filled with more people
    • “stational worship” (Baldovin)
      • worship at different stations (churches, shrines)
      • thematically related to those places
    • processions
  • transition to legalization
    • example: burning of incense
      • early: would never do it, people martryred for not burning incense before image of Caesar
      • after 4C: becomes integral part of Christian worship
    • from adult to infant baptism
    • delayed baptism or penance
  • why? rise of “mysteriological piety” (Schmemann)
    • impress/awe complacent “social Christians”
    • (compare to contemporary megachurch worship?)
    • gold, art, icons, architecture
    • splendorous kinds of music - more presentational than participatory
  • Example: Contrast Oxyrhynchus hymn (3rd century) with contemporary Byzantine (Greek Orthodox) chant
