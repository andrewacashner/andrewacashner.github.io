Tensions/paradoxes in medieval West

  • state recognition/sponsorship allows greater artistic freedom, creativity in worship
  • but also tendency to specialized liturgy not intelligible to commoners

  • emphasis on impressive aesthetics rather than church mission?

  • divisions of social class, religious orders & rank, literate vs. illiterate, women vs. men
    • female monasteries (convents) as unique space for women
    • escape from dangers of childbirth, labors of home
    • opportunity for literacy, creative expression

    • “married to Jesus”

    • increasingly high concept of God – as above human troubles, above change, above feeling; also of Jesus
    • Jesus : from mediator between God and humanity – to judge
    • Mary and saints come to seen in mediator role

    • expansion of hierarchy, also on human level: bishop of Rome -> pope; archbishops, cardinals, etc.

    • general sense of awe and wonder, not understanding powers greater than human knowledge

  • Carolingian reform (Charlemagne, Charles the Great, Holy Roman Emperor)
    • based partly on papal ceremonial books
    • addition of Frankish creativity – exuberance, flamboyance, expressive
      • vs. Romans: “soberness and sense”
    • combination of ancient Roman core, Frankish embellishments, and more recent, elaborate Roman ceremony; fused together into complex medieval rite

Medieval worship music types

Plainchant (Gregorian chant)

  • Example: Alleluia ‘Christus resurgens’
    • melismatic (more than one note per syllable; vs. syllabic)
  • Salve Regina
    • widespread devotional chant to Virgin Mary
    • turning to mother for comfort in times of crisis and pain; Mary as “our Advocate”, in between humans and Jesus
    • looking to heaven, world to come; dark view of this world as place of suffering
