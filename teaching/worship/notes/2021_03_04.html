Perotin - Notre Dame polyphony (12th century, Paris)

  • faster tempo - steady beat, regular rhythmic pattern
  • more interesting, dynamic
  • multiple voices
  • drone?
  • voices imitating each other
  • a capella - voices only
    • but with organ pedal
  • extremely stretched out lyrics
    • effect: like no lyrics, just sounds
    • melismatic

Notre Dame cathedral

  • windows, stained glass - emphasis on color and light
  • spacious, long and tall
    • acoustical effect
    • analagous to the “musical architecture” of polyphony
  • dialectic between impression of the whole and particular details

Effect on listeners?

Ockeghem - Missa prolationum (15C, Burgundy)

  • canon (law) - form of imitative counterpoint governed by a rule
  • mensuration canon - each voice has same notes, but different rhythmic/metrical pattern
    • result is multi-voice harmony from a single melody
  • intense patterning, puzzles, enigmas - “God sees it”
  • hidden harmony, hidden order in the world

Josquin des Prez - Tu pauperum refugium (16C, Burgundy)

  • influence of Renaissance Humanism
    • after rediscovery of Greek and Roman “classics”, new desire among artists, writers, musicians, to convey human experience in a realistic way
    • new desire to use music to communicate in a (somewhat) more rational way; reduced interest in music as awe-inducing puzzle (as in previous example); though both ideas coexist
  • influence of radical Franciscan movement
    • St. Francis wanted the church to serve the poor, return to a primitive Christianity modeled directly on the teachings of Jesus
    • Franciscans advocated radical reform of the church, to clear away abuses, recenter on a mission of love and service to the poor
  • influence of Devotio moderna (cf. Thomas a Kempis)
    • movement centered in the Low Countries (Netherlands), 15th cent.
    • revival of medieval mystical theology (emphasis on spiritual union with God) together with Franciscan-style emphasis on primitive, simple Christianity, “The Imitation of Christ” in his poverty and suffering
    • cultivated practices of meditation, especially on the suffering of Christ
