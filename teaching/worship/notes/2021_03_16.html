Primitive Christianity (-ies?)

Medieval Christianity in the East

- Eastern Orthodoxy

Medieval Christianity in the West

- penitential piety (emphasis on sin and salvation, heaven and hell)
- Eucharistic piety (but differences of participation)
- hierarchical authority structure (centered in Rome)

Lutherans

- followers of Martin Luther
- centered in Germany and Scandinavia

- Luther: Augustinian friar, trained as lawyer
- Law and Gospel (especially from Romans in NT)
    - Law: you are a sinner, you cannot save yourself through good works
      or otherwise; your debt/crime is infinite
    - Gospel (good news): Christ is your savior, he has paid it all, his
      infinite merit avails for your sin, you are saved on account of his
      righteousness
        - equivalent to "the Word"
- "solas"   
    - Sinful humans are saved by God's grace alone through Christ alone by
      faith alone, on the basis of Scripture alone
    - sola gratia, sola Christi, sola fide, sola Scriptura
- **justification** by grace

- 95 theses against indulgences (1517)

- On the Freedom of the Christian Person (1520)
- On the Babylonian Captivity of the Church
- Appeal to the German Nobility

- Translation of the New Testament, Old into German

- Catholic emphasis: authority

- attitudes about worship
    - that which is not forbidden, may be permitted as long as it does not
      obscure the gospel
    - "abuse does not merit disuse"
    - both/and
    - conservative by comparison to other Protestant churches

“Reformed”/Presbyterians

- early: Uldritch Zwingli 
- later: Jean Calvin
- centered in Switzerland, France, and later Scotland, New England
    - Scotland: John Knox
    - New England: (for example) Jonathan Edwards

Anglicans/Church of England/Episcopalians

- national church centered on English monarch
- political reform primarily
- main leader for worship reform: Thomas Cranmer, Archbishop of Canterbury
- *Book of Common Prayer*
- centered in Britain and New England

“Radical” Reformed (“left-wing”)

- including Anabaptists (ancestors of today's "Baptists")
- "enthusiasts" (ancestors of today's Charismatics)
- Mennonites, Amish

- Quakers/Religious Society of Friends
    - worship in complete silence
    - no ministers, no titles, no symbols, no creeds
    - wait for the "light within"
    - pacifists
    - governed by consensus
  • later: Methodists, Evangelicals, charismatics
