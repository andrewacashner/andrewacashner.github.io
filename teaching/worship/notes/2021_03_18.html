Paradoxes in Luther’s theology

  • Old vs. New Testament (Scriptures), old vs. new covenant (system of salvation)
    • relationship with the Jewish people
    • lumping together Jewish (OT) law with contemporary Catholic regulations (e.g., fasting, celibacy)
  • simul justus et peccator (at once justified and a sinner)
  • assurance of salvation vs. constant need for forgiveness and faith
  • constant need for forgiveness vs. idea that God makes us holy
  • “you have to preach them into hell before you can preach them into heaven” (J. Walther, Law and Gospel, 19th cent.)

Lutheran worship emphases

  • conveying Trost (comfort)
  • proclaiming The WORD
    • word as sacrament: powerful means through which God’s grace enters human lives

  • embodying the good news

  • conservative w/r/t tradition: keep what works and doesn’t obscure the gospel

  • adiaphora – indifferent things: not specified in Scripture, therefore you have freedom

  • remove what does obscure the gospel
    • anything about sacrifice (esp. Mass)
    • anything about praying to the saints (only Christ is the mediator)
  • vernacular language (German), but not exclusively
    • both/and

  • strong emphasis on education, city Latin boys’ schools with choirs

  • vernacular hymns for congregational singing
    • teach
    • comfort
    • allow you to worship
  • all kinds of music (instrumental, choral) based on the hymn tunes
    • reinforcement
  • “church, home, and school”

  • Hausandacht - home devotions

  • hymn books

Hymns

Es ist das Heil uns kommen her

  • Music: “Bar” form, AABA
  • doctrine of Law and Gospel
  • truth over beauty (beauty is still important)

Aus tiefer Noth

  • Music and words by Martin Luther
  • based on Psalm 130
  • transformed into a law/gospel message
  • but more devotional focus

Erhalt uns Herr, bei deinem Wort

  • Music and words by Martin Luther
  • embattled outlook: “Protect us from the murderousness of the pope and the Turk who want to wrest Jesus Christ from his throne”
