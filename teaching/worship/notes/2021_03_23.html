Lutheranism review

  • Vom Himmel hoch da komm ich her (Luther’s Christmas hymn)
    • “glad tidings of great joy” - angel’s message of Jesus’s birth
    • spreading the good news (gospel, Evangelium)
      • Jesus is savior from sin
      • personal connection to Jesus
    • tune by Martin Luther (end of melody = Ein’ feste Burg)
    • in reconstructed version by Paul McCreesh & Gabrieli Consort
      • alternating verses (alternatim)
        • solo voice, unaccompanied
        • solo voice, accompanied by organ, instruments (krummhorns, shawms/oboe, sackbuts/trombone, strings, harpsichord)
        • full choir + organ, instruments + CONGREGATION
          • choir and instruments in harmony; congregation is in unison
          • choir located behind and/or to sides of congregation; organ behind, all facing forward
        • congregation alone, monophonic (unaccompanied) - most common
        • organ prelude (“chorale prelude”) - introducing
    • Lutheran values on display:
      • music follows the Word
      • bonus, not superfluous (adiaphora, indifferent matters in which there is freedom)
      • both/and approach to tradition
      • congregational participation
        • individual relationship to God
        • individual faith: faith as individual trust in Christ
        • emphasis on education, esp. rote learning

The Reformed (Calvinists, Presbyterians)

  • Uldritch Zwingli
    • Switzerland
    • radical (radix, root)
    • just preach the Bible
      • preaching service possibly derived from medieval “prone” service of preaching before Mass

    • just believe in the gospel

    • sacraments (baptism, Eucharist), “ordinances” (Jesus told us to do it), “memorials” (Jesus said “do this in remembrance of me”)

  • Jean Calvin (1509-1564)
    • French Catholic priest turned Protestant reformer
    • wants to finish Luther’s work, systematize, streamline, rationalize, clean up, order
    • exiled to Switzerland
    • Geneva - establishes theocracy/dictatorship

    • Institutes of the Christian Religion - systematic theology

    • “that which is not commanded in Scripture should be forbidden”

    • sovereignty of God
    • total depravity of humankind
    • salvation is a sovereign act of God by God’s will
      • so is damnation
    • -> predestination (double predestination)
      • God created some people in order to save them and others, in order to damn them
      • election (being chosen) - saved = “the elect”
    • church is the community of the elect; the true church in every age is invisible

    • you can never know whether you are among the elect

    • faith in Christ is itself a gift of God’s grace
    • you have faith because God is saving you

    • covenant theology - love that will not let you go

    • church governance
      • governed by elders (presbyters) who sit in “session”
      • elected representatives
      • vote within session
      • congregational model, each church independentA
    • self-policing community
      • elect will demonstrate the fruits of the spirit
      • notorious sinners would be banned (excommunicated) from the “table” (communion) until they publicly repented
      • legalistic emphasis (despite theology)

    • worship: preaching, repentance, music: unaccompanied singing of Biblical psalms
