Calvin on sacraments

  • Signs, symbols of the covenant
    • Baptism
      • including infant baptism

    • Lord’s Supper

    • reminders, memorial (Cf. Zwingli)

    • sign of God’s covenant love, and of human covenant to God (especially baptism)

    • Christ’s body is not brought down to earth; church is brought up to heaven to be with Christ
    • Christ is present in virtus (“virtually” present)
      • root: vir (Latin, man)
      • manliness, personhood, power, strength, ~essence
      • holiness, good attributes, morality

Reformed worship

Preface to Calvin’s Forme of Prayers

  • Christ is more important than ceremonies
  • ceremonies should be based on Scripture

  • Scriptures command: preaching, sacraments, prayer

  • singing: good gift of God, commanded in Scripture, established in early church
    • from the heart, in praise to God, together
    • especially psalms
  • but Roman church especially has corrupted it (really Satan’s doing)
    • “strange language, that can not edifie”
    • using it not in connection with the Word of God, and its intended purpose
    • “hyringe men to tickle the eares, and flatter the phantasies”
  • what to sing? “there are no songes more meete, then the psalmes of the Prophete Dauid”
    • Calvin, Commentary on the Psalms: the psalms are the Bible’s prayer-book, they contain every movement of the heart
    • in verse/meter

Psalms

  • Metrical psalms - vernacular translation, in regular verse forms - rhythm and rhyme

    • Example: Psalm 18: 8.6.8.6 (syllable count)

    • common meters:
      • Common Meter, Short Meter, Long Meter
  • common tunes to fit the meters

  • sung monophonically (in unison)

  • Genevan Psalter
    • French words by Clement Marot, Theodore de Beze

    • Tunes by Louis Bourgeous

    • polyphonic settings by Claude Goudimel
      • to be sung at home only
  • Scottish (Gaelic) psalter for Reformed churches

  • English psalter both for Reformed churches and used by Anglican parish (smaller, rural) church communities

Performance practice

  • “Old Way of Singing”
    • unruly, coarse; added embellishments, slides, etc.
    • singing by memory, not from notation
  • opening note: gathering tone
  • “lining out”
    • precentor (song leader) introduce each line before the people respond
