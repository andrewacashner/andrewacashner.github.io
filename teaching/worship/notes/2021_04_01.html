St. Matthew Passion

The Passion of Our Lord and Savior Jesus Christ according to Saint Matthew

  • Johann Sebastian Bach (1685–1750)
  • St. Thomas Church (Thomaskirche), Leipzig, Germany
  • Premiered 1729

Reading the story of Jesus’s suffering (passion) and death from one of the four gospels during Holy Week

Holy Week: the week leading up to Easter/Resurrection Sunday

  • Palm Sunday
    • Jesus’s triumphal entry into Jerusalem
    • Procession with palm branches
    • Reading of the Passion (according to John?)
  • Maundy Thursday
    • The Last Supper in the upper room (Matthew, Mark, Luke)
      • sharing bread and wine
    • Jesus washing the disciples’ feet (John)
    • Mandatum – command
      • “Do this in remembrance of me”
      • “A new command I give you, that you love one another as I have loved you”

    • Communion plus foot-washing ceremony

    • Jesus prays in the Garden of Gethsemane
    • Jesus is arrested – identified/betrayed by Judas Iscariot

    • night trial: first before Jewish authorities, then the Roman authorities

  • Good Friday
    • Jesus is crucified
    • Reading of Passion from Matthew

    • Veneration of the cross

    • Jesus is buried in a tomb

  • Holy Saturday (= Sabbath)

  • Vigil of Easter (night before)
    • recounts whole history of salvation
    • themes of darkness and light
    • baptism
  • Resurrection Sunday (Easter)
    • “he is not here, he is risen, go and tell the others”

Singing the passion

Medieval tradition

  • Roles
    • Evangelist: narrator
    • Jesus: words of Jesus
    • Chorus: Turba (crowd, disciples)
    • Pilate: words of Pilate
    • Synagoga: words of Jewish high priests, authorities
  • long tradition of anti-Semitism in Holy Week, esp. Good Friday
    • license for anti-Jewish violence
  • connection with dramatic re-enactments of the story
    • still today, e.g., Ixtapalapa, Mexico; Philippines

Lutheran passions & Bach

  • normal, weekly practice in 18th century Lutheranism of cantata before & after sermon

Elements

  • Narrative (ACTION)
    • Recitative
      • Evangelist (tenor), Jesus (bass)
      • unaccompanied (evangelist, etc.)
      • unaccompanied with “halo” of strings for Jesus
    • Turba chorus
  • Recitative–Aria pairs (REFLECTION)
    • personalizes, models individual devotion

    • intense emotion

    • (cf. Greek chorus)

    • model of contemporary opera

    • Recitative (often accompanied)

    • Aria = song; instrumental introduction; ABA “da capo”

  • Chorale (REFLECTION)
    • well-known hymn texts and tunes
    • probably not sung by congregation but represent the congregation’s response
  • Chorus (REFLECTION)
    • more elaborate work for choir
    • can include chorales
