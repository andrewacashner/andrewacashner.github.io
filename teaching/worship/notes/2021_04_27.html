Beginnings of Cultural Shifts in 20th-Cent. American Worship

Andrew Cashner

2021/04/27

Back to schedule

  • mainstreams of music/worship practice in 20C North America
    1. mainline white-majority Protestant churches – big choral/organ programs
      • upper-class, community/social institutions
      • “nice music program”
        • paid quartet of soloists
        • organist with a big pipe organ
        • post-WWII choral/educational program
      • formal, respectable
      • social issues hidden or ignored (e.g., race, sexuality)
      • professional music leaders, not necessarily church members
      • hymns, sung straight; choir anthems
        • example: John Rutter, For the Beauty of the Earth (c. 1980)
      • was its own market for music publishing and sales
    2. Black (Protestant, Baptist) churches
      • emphasis on choir, organ (electronic, Hammond B-3), band, and vocal leaders
      • rise of Black Gospel genre - Thomas Dorsey
        • blend of African American worship traditions with blues, R&B (themselves already influenced by Black worship traditions)
        • Edwin Hawkins, 1968 O Happy Day
        • emergence of a commercial market for gospel music
        • controversies and concerns about secular vs. sacred music (variously defined)
    3. Roman Catholic churches
      • liturgical movement
        • 1st stage, medievalist revival movement (Golden Age: 13th century)
          • monks of the abbey of Solesmes
            • reviving Gregorian chant
          • Vatican I (1869)
            • retrenchment against modernity
            • papal infallibility
        • 2nd stage, early church (Golden Age: esp. 4th century)
          • Vatican II (1962)
            • relaxed restrictions on vernacular
            • openness to change, adaptability, modernization
          • 1969 revision of the liturgy
            • three-year lectionary of Scripture readings covering almost all of Scripture
              • instead of an epistle and a gospel with a short chant between, now: Old Testament, Psalm, Epistle, Gospel Acclamation/Hymn, Gospel reading
          • responsorial psalm singing
            • cantor who sings most of the text, congregation sings a refrain (“antiphon”)
          • emphasis on lay participation
          • example, Michael Joncas, On Eagles’ Wings (Psalm 91 with refrain from Isaiah)
            • music of St. Louis Jesuits
          • what would vernacular music sound like?
            • “folk masses”
